Tokenization is an important step in natural language processing.
Byte Pair Encoding, also known as BPE, is a subword tokenization algorithm.
It starts with a vocabulary of individual characters and iteratively merges the most frequent pairs.
This allows the model to handle rare words and out of vocabulary tokens effectively.
Modern language models such as GPT and RoBERTa use BPE based tokenizers.
The tokenizer learns merge rules from a training corpus.
Each merge rule combines two symbols into a new symbol.
The process repeats until the desired vocabulary size is reached.
Subword tokenization balances vocabulary size and sequence length.
Smaller vocabularies produce longer sequences and larger vocabularies produce shorter sequences.
Tokenizers are a core part of any natural language processing pipeline.
A good tokenizer improves model accuracy and training efficiency.
